# Growth Playbook — Scoopd
**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Goal:** Move average GSC position from ~55 to page 1 for target keywords. Drive meaningful traffic.

---

## Context

- 192 restaurants tracked, 10 blog posts live, all SEO technical work complete (Phase 4)
- Average GSC position: mid-50s. Zero clicks in first 2 months.
- Root cause: not technical, not content — domain authority is zero. No external backlinks.
- Data journalism angle exists but requires editorial framing, not automated metrics.
- Difficulty metric is defensible as editorial judgment from observed booking behavior.
- Claude time (not human time) is the primary constraint.

---

## What This Is Not

- Not a feature build — no code written under this plan
- Not a data journalism play requiring automated difficulty metrics
- Not a concierge outreach program (Phase 5, after reputation is established)
- Not a social-first strategy (social follows press, not the other way)

---

## Track 1: Reddit Engagement

**Goal:** Immediate real traffic from people actively searching for reservation help.

**Why:** r/FoodNYC, r/AskNYC, and r/finedining receive constant questions about how to book specific NYC restaurants. The six how-to posts answer exactly those questions. A knowledgeable answer with a natural link to the relevant post drives clicks and builds brand familiarity.

**Execution:**
- Claude builds a library of 20-30 pre-written answers targeting the most common reservation questions across the three subreddits.
- Each answer is genuinely useful and complete — not a teaser requiring a click.
- Links to Scoopd posts are woven in naturally, not as the lead.
- Voice: personal, first-person, not brand. The aged personal account is the vehicle.
- User pastes and posts. Time cost: 2 minutes per post.

**What Claude delivers:**
- 20-30 answer templates organized by restaurant and question type
- Targeting notes for each (which subreddit, what question pattern to watch for)
- Refresh set every 2-3 months as new posts go live

**Success metric:** Real referral traffic from Reddit within 2 weeks of first posts.

---

## Track 2: Press Placement

**Goal:** One or two backlinks from high-authority food media domains. This is the primary ranking lever.

**Why:** Domain authority comes from external sites linking to the domain. A single link from Eater NY, Grub Street, or The Cut carries more ranking signal than months of content work. The site has two pieces already written that are pitch-ready.

### Piece 1: "Who Gets the Table"

The cultural history piece (`/blog/who-gets-the-table`) is the primary press vehicle.

- Already complete, no Scoopd self-promotion in the body
- Ends cleanly on a substantive line ("the price now accrues to a financial institution rather than a man with a pencil")
- Research is specific, sources are named, arc from Chumley's to Amex is original
- Reads as independent cultural journalism, not product marketing

**Submission format:** Pitch as a finished guest piece. Author bio: 2 sentences, includes "founder of Scoopd.nyc." The byline is the backlink.

**Targets (in priority order):**
1. Eater NY — Voices section, publishes op-eds and cultural criticism
2. Grub Street (New York Magazine) — publishes long-form food culture
3. The Cut (New York Magazine) — broader culture angle fits
4. NYC food newsletters (Nikita Richardson, Robert Sietsema)

**Claude delivers:** Pitch email for each outlet, tailored to editorial voice. User sends.

### Piece 2: "The Reservation Economy"

Secondary press vehicle. More opinionated, shorter, better suited to newsletter or column format.

**Required edit:** Strip final two paragraphs (Scoopd product pitch). Replace with a neutral conclusion landing on the same idea — that the information to compete fairly was always there, the problem was access to it.

**Targets:** Different outlets than Piece 1. Food newsletters, Substack food writers with engaged audiences who might link or share.

**Claude delivers:** Rewritten conclusion + pitch email. User sends.

---

## Track 3: How-To Content — Next Tier

**Goal:** Cover the remaining restaurants with genuinely non-obvious booking mechanics.

**Why:** "How to get a reservation at [name]" is a high-intent search query. The format earns its place only when the restaurant has mechanics worth explaining — a platform switch, non-standard inventory, a fee structure, walk-in culture, monthly drop conflicts.

**Cap:** 10-20 posts total across all how-to content. After that, the remaining restaurants follow a standard pattern ("get on Resy at X AM, click, done") that doesn't warrant a dedicated guide.

**Prioritization criteria (in order):**
1. Difficulty level: Very Hard and Extremely Hard first
2. Name recognition: national search demand
3. Non-obvious mechanics: platform switches, non-standard inventory, monthly drop conflicts, walk-in programs, fee structures

**Candidate list (not exhaustive, user selects):**
Le Bernardin, Eleven Madison Park, Per Se, Masa, Joo Ok, Aska, Corner Store, Or'Esh, Jean-Georges, Gramercy Tavern

**Execution:** Batched 5 at a time during normal sessions. Each post links internally to relevant neighborhood pages, the rolling-windows post, and the-reservation-economy where applicable.

**After ~20 posts:** How-to format is exhausted. Ongoing content shifts to editorial pieces in the style of "Who Gets the Table" — original argument, cultural or data angle, harder to write but higher value for press placement.

---

## Execution Order

1. Reddit library (Track 1) — first, delivers traffic immediately, low Claude cost
2. "Who Gets the Table" pitch (Track 2) — draft submission email, user sends
3. "Reservation Economy" rewrite (Track 2) — strip ending, write neutral conclusion, draft pitch
4. Next how-to batch (Track 3) — 5 posts, user selects restaurants
5. Repeat Track 3 until ~20 posts total
6. Ongoing: new editorial pieces as angles emerge; new Reddit templates as new posts go live

---

## What This Does Not Include (and Why)

- **Concierge outreach:** Traffic play, not a backlink play. Concierges recommend what guests already use. Revisit in Phase 5 after traffic is established.
- **Social-first:** Social follows reputation. Building an audience before press coverage is the slower path.
- **Data journalism with automated metrics:** Difficulty metric is editorial judgment, not algorithmic. Defensible as-is for the press pieces. Automated data journalism requires Phase 5 crowdsourced booking outcomes.
- **Product Hunt / Hacker News:** High-variance, requires timing and community momentum. Not sequenced here.
