[Joo Ok](/restaurant/joo-ok) is a two-Michelin-star Korean tasting menu restaurant in [Koreatown](/neighborhood/koreatown). The restaurant has 32 seats. There is no walk-in program, no bar seating, and no secondary path. You book it or you do not eat here.

## The drop: midnight

This is the detail that most people miss. Reservations release on Resy at midnight on the first of every month -- not 9 AM, not 10 AM, but the exact moment the 1st begins. This is a [monthly drop](/blog/rolling-windows-and-monthly-drops), and it opens at the start of the day, not during business hours.

Set an alarm for 11:55 PM on the last day of the month. Be inside the Resy app before midnight. Have your date and party size ready. When the inventory appears, book immediately.

Your Resy account needs to be fully set up before midnight -- payment info saved, account active and confirmed. At midnight you should be clicking, not creating an account or adding a credit card. A 10 AM drop gives you time to sort that out on the morning of; midnight gives you none.

## Three months out

Joo Ok's window is three absolute months out, which is further than most comparable restaurants. On June 1 at midnight, September reservations open. On July 1 at midnight, October opens. The extra month of runway sounds like more opportunity, but the 32-seat capacity means competition is proportionally high regardless of how far out the window extends.

## The restaurant

Chef Chang-ho Shin earned two Michelin stars in Seoul before bringing Joo Ok to New York. This is not a branch of a Seoul restaurant. It is the restaurant. The format is a 12-course tasting menu. The menu changes seasonally.

32 seats is a small room by any measure. One party, one night, one bad booking experience and the table is gone for a month. The structure of the restaurant -- no walk-ins, no alternative format, no secondary seating -- means every seat is booked through that midnight window on the 1st.

## Missing the drop

If you miss the 1st, you are waiting until next month. There is no rolling window to check, no same-day cancellation culture to monitor, no phone line that surfaces hidden availability. You can watch Resy for genuine cancellations, but at 32 seats, they are rare. The 1st of the month is the realistic path.
