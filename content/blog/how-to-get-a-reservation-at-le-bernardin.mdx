[Le Bernardin](/restaurant/le-bernardin) is a three-star French seafood restaurant in [Midtown](/neighborhood/midtown). It has held that Michelin distinction longer than almost any restaurant in New York. The reservation is hard but not unwinnable. If you understand the release system, you have a real shot.

## The drop

Reservations release on Resy on the first of every month at 7 AM. This is a [monthly drop](/blog/rolling-windows-and-monthly-drops), not a rolling window. On June 1, August reservations open. On July 1, September opens. If you miss the 1st, you are waiting 30 days for another chance.

7 AM is earlier than most comparable restaurants. Per Se and Eleven Madison Park both drop at 10 AM. Le Bernardin drops at 7. Set an alarm, be inside the Resy app before the clock turns, and have your party size, date, and time preference ready. The moment the inventory appears, start booking. Don't browse.

## Hard, not Very Hard

Le Bernardin's difficulty is Hard -- not Very Hard -- which means being prepared at 7 AM on the 1st is a realistic path to a reservation for most nights. You are not competing against an impossible field. If you show up ready and move fast, you will get a table.

Weekend dinner is tighter than weekday dinner, as it is at every restaurant of this caliber. If you can go on a Tuesday or Wednesday, you will have a noticeably easier time on the 1st. Weekday lunch is the lowest-pressure window at Le Bernardin and a legitimate way to experience the kitchen.

## The bar lounge

If you miss the drop or the dates don't align, Le Bernardin has a bar and lounge area with a lighter menu -- a separate experience from the formal dining room but the same kitchen. It is walk-in friendly and you can often get a seat without a reservation. The menu is abbreviated, but it is not a consolation prize. It is a real way to eat here.

## What to expect when you arrive

Le Bernardin operates a prix-fixe format for the main dining room. Service is formal and paced. There is no ordering around; the restaurant is designed to be a long meal. Build the evening around it. If you are interested in a comparable monthly-drop experience in a different setting, the guides for [Per Se](/blog/how-to-get-a-reservation-at-per-se) and [Eleven Madison Park](/blog/how-to-get-a-reservation-at-eleven-madison-park) cover two other three-star restaurants that operate on the same calendar logic.
