[Masa](/restaurant/masa) is a three-Michelin-star Japanese omakase restaurant in [Columbus Circle](/neighborhood/columbus-circle). It is consistently cited as the most expensive restaurant in America. The omakase begins above $1,000 per person before beverages. That number is relevant context, not a selling point. Budget for it before you book.

## The platform: Tock

Masa books through Tock, not Resy or OpenTable. If you are looking on either of those platforms, you will find nothing. Create a Tock account at exploretock.com before the drop date. Add your payment information. Confirm the account works. Do not wait until the morning of the 1st.

## The drop: midnight

Reservations release on Tock at midnight on the first of every month, two absolute months out. This is a [monthly drop](/blog/rolling-windows-and-monthly-drops). On June 1 at midnight, August opens. On July 1 at midnight, September opens.

Midnight is not a typo. The window opens when the calendar turns, not during business hours. Set an alarm for 11:55 PM on the last day of the month. Be logged into Tock and ready. When inventory appears, book.

Have your Tock account created, payment method saved, and Masa favorited before midnight. At $1,000+ per person, you do not want to be typing a credit card number when the window opens.

## No substitutions

Masa is an omakase. There is no menu selection, no course skipping, and no substitutions. The meal is what it is on the night you go. If you or someone in your party has a food restriction that cannot accommodate an omakase format, this is not the right booking. That is not a judgment -- it is information to have before the $1,000-per-person charge processes.

## The counter and the room

Masa seats guests at a hinoki wood counter. The counter is sanded daily. The plateware is designed by Masayoshi Takayama. The physical environment -- the wood, the materials, the spatial arrangement -- is part of the experience, not incidental to it. There is no outdoor seating and no walk-in bar. You are here for the counter or you are not here.

## Per Se is in the same building

Per Se is in the same building -- Deutsche Bank Center at Columbus Circle -- and also books through Tock on the 1st of every month at 10 AM. If you have a Tock account for Masa, you are already set up to book Per Se. The guide for [Per Se](/blog/how-to-get-a-reservation-at-per-se) covers that drop and what to expect from the tasting menu format.
